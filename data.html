--- layout: page title: Data --- Through our experiments we investigated existance and impact of atoms of confusion. Through reverse engineering winners of the International Obfuscated C Code Contest, we generated a partial list of 19 potential atoms. In the atom existence experiment we tested how well subjects could hand trace those atoms compared to functionally equivalent pieces of code with the obfuscations removed. Of the 19 proposed atoms, 15 were statistically confirmed to be signficantly confusing to our subjects.
